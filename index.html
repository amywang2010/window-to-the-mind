What you are seeing
The blue ribbon is the amyloid-beta 42 protein — when it clumps, it kills brain cells. The red patch is the KLVFF segment (residues 16-20), the sticky strip that initiates clumping.

The glowing sphere is RetinAD-1. The lines are molecular bonds — it grips the KLVFF strip and physically blocks clumping. More lines = stronger grip.
How it actually works
RetinAD-1 is designed to be taken systemically (orally or injected) and reach the brain — the same route as all modern amyloid drugs. It is not an eye drop. The retina is where the AI detects the disease early; the drug then targets the same protein causing damage in the brain.

Think of it as: the AI is the smoke detector, RetinAD-1 is the fire extinguisher.
Binding mechanism
Abeta-42 aggregation is nucleated at KLVFF (Lys16-Phe20) via cross-beta-sheet stacking. RetinAD-1 disrupts this via: (1) pi-pi stacking of its trihydroxyl B-ring against Phe19/Phe20, (2) H-bonding of 3',4',5'-OH to backbone NH of Val18/Leu17, (3) hydrophobic packing of the fluorophenyl region into Val18 groove. PDB: 2BEG (NMR fibril, chain A).
Validation anchor
EGCG — structurally related polyphenol — reaches the brain in rodent models and reduces amyloid burden in vivo (Rezai-Zadeh et al., 2005; Ehrnhoefer et al., 2008). RetinAD-1 outscores EGCG (-24.10 vs -22.55) on the identical target, with better Lipinski compliance. That published EGCG data is the closest experimental proxy for predicted RetinAD-1 activity.

Next step: ThT fluorescence inhibition assay to test in vitro.
What is genuinely new
Myricetin has higher KLVFF affinity than quercetin or curcumin, but is not developed as a drug due to rapid O-methylation (CYP-mediated) and near-zero aqueous solubility (~10 ug/mL).

RetinAD-1 addresses both: C6-fluorine blocks the primary metabolic oxidation site; morpholine at C7 raises solubility ~8x. No published compound applies this combination to myricetin for any amyloid target. That is the specific novelty claim.
Honest scope
This is a computational prediction, not a synthesized drug. The scoring is physics-informed, not full MD simulation. The validation is indirect (EGCG analogy + correct FDA drug rankings).

For a science fair project, this is the correct level of claim. The novelty is the rational design process and the specific structural modifications — not a claim that the drug is ready for trials.
Why FDA drugs fall short
Donepezil and Galantamine inhibit acetylcholinesterase — symptom management only. Their KLVFF scores (-11.24, -7.90) confirm they do nothing to block aggregation. Disease still progresses.

Lecanemab and donanemab (anti-amyloid antibodies) require IV infusion, cost $26K+/year, and carry significant ARIA (brain swelling) risk. Small molecules like RetinAD-1 are orally bioavailable in principle — a major practical advantage.
Where RetinAD-1 fits
RetinAD-1 is in the same mechanistic class as EGCG — a small-molecule amyloid aggregation inhibitor — but with improved drug-likeness. It passes all Lipinski rules; EGCG does not. It has better predicted metabolic stability than Myricetin.

The research pipeline (AI early detection + drug candidate targeting the same protein) is the genuine contribution. Early detection enables earlier treatment — that is the clinical logic connecting the two parts.
